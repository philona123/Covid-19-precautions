How to stay safe from Covid-19?

Covid-19 is an ongoing pandemic caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2. The outbreak was first identified in Wuhan, Hubei, China in DEcember 2019 and recognized as a pandemic by the World Health Organisation (WHO) on 11 March 2020. As of now, more than 183,000 cases of the disease have been reported in over 160 countries and territories, resulting in more than 7,100 deaths and around 79,000 recoveries.

How does covid-19 spread from person to person?

  • Being a respiratory disease, it's transmitted through respiratory droplets.
  • Through coughing and sneezing.
  • Shaking hands with infected person unknowingly.
  • Using the things like hair combs, bath towels etc of an infected person.

What are the symptoms?

  1. Fever.
  2. Body aches.
  3. Coughing.
  4. Nasal congestion.
  5. Runny nose.
  6. Sore throat.
  7. Shortness of breath.

Who all are at a higher risk for Covid-19?

  • Those who are having low immunity like old aged people, newborn babies and children.
  • Those with Chronic medical conditions.
  • Those who have travelled to covid-19 affected countries.

How can we prevent ourselves from being infected by Covid-19?

  • Wash your hands frequently. Clean your hands with an alcohol-based hand sanitizer or wash them with soap and water.
  • Maintain social distancing. Maintain at least 1 meter distance between yorself and anyone who is coughing and sneezing.
  • Avoid touching eyes, nose and mouth. Once contaminated, hands can transfer the virus to your eyes, nose or mouth.
  • If you have any symptoms seek medical care early. Stay home if you feel unwell. If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention and call in advance.
  • Stay informed and follow advice given by your healthcare provider. Follow advice given by your healthcare provider, your national and local public health authority and follow WHO website for the updated and information
  • Stay away from wrong informations. Don't follow the fake instructions that are forwarded through social medias.
  • Do not panic. Be alert and do not panic. Help others to keep vigilance and be safe. Take care!
